Human Speed Detector

Perfect for accurate results in fitness tests!

Inspiration

As a student who has participated in 4 years worth of fitness tests, the 100m dash is something I genuinely look forward to. But for every 100m dash until now, the timing has been done the same way, with a person manually operating a stopwatch at the finish line. This is why I feel like the idea of implementing a human speed detector would prove to be far more efficient and productive.”

Basic Components

  • 2 sensors to detect crossing
  • Circuit board with wires, resistors, and connecting wires
  • LCD display screen
  • Battery or USB power supply

How does it work?

It uses two sensors that are placed at a known distance apart (like 1 meter). When a person passes the first sensor, it records the time. When the person passes the second sensor, it records the second time. By knowing the distance and calculating the time difference, it finds the speed (Speed = Distance ÷ Time).

Who will use it?

  • Students: For science or physics projects
  • Coaches: To measure athlete speeds
  • Schools: For fitness tests
  • Anyone: Curious about measuring walking or running speeds
