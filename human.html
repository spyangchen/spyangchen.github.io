Human Speed Detector

Perfect for accurate results in fitness tests!

Inspiration

Schools conduct fitness tests five times a year to assess students' physical abilities. One key event is the 100-meter sprint, but the results are often inaccurate due to manual timing errors like slow reaction times or inconsistent stopwatch use. To solve this problem, the human speed detector can be used to automatically and precisely measure running speed, ensuring fair and reliable results every time.

Basic Components

  • 2 sensors to detect crossing
  • Circuit board with wires, resistors, and connecting wires
  • LCD display screen
  • Battery or USB power supply

How does it work?

It uses two sensors that are placed at a known distance apart (like 1 meter). When a person passes the first sensor, it records the time. When the person passes the second sensor, it records the second time. By knowing the distance and calculating the time difference, it finds the speed (Speed = Distance รท Time).

Who will use it?

  • Students: For science or physics projects
  • Coaches: To measure athlete speeds
  • Schools: For fitness tests
  • Anyone: Curious about measuring walking or running speeds
